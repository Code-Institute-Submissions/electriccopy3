UK Household Electric Consumption between 1970 - 2015
We in the UK utilize electricity as an everyday necessity . . .
But do we know where it is most used within our households

The following shows on average where the electricity is consumed within the average UK household.

We may be on different tarifs but you should be aware of where the highest consumption takes place. The charts show consumption not cost because people will be on differing tarifs with different energy companies and may possibly have solar panels to produce their own electricity.

The charts display the electricity used in households across the UK.

Please see the section at the bottom for details of where the data for the charts was obtained.

With changes in technology ie. energy saving bulbs and LEDs etc what was a high consuming area may now not be so high.

The charts below show changes over 45 years.

Previously there were no computers, microwaves and game consoles in the 1970's. Now in 2015 these exist, but also so do energy saving devices.

Where has the charts and data for the charts come from?

The data used to create the charts came from the UK government website. Taken from one sheet from a very large Excel spreadsheet.

The web site it was taken from was www.gov.uk with the title Gov.UK
    In the Contents section choose Statistics
    Headed National Statistics Energy consumption in the UK
    Under documents choose Energy Consumption in the UK (ECUK) 2017 Data Tables
    Select the spreadsheet was ECUK_Tables_2017.xlsx Sheet reference 3.08
    The table within the spreadsheet was Table 3.08 Total electricity consumption by household domestic appliances 1970 to 2015

    Source

    Market Transformation Programme and analysis by Cambridge Architectural Research Ltd


The only modification to the data has been to enable it to be read to produce the charts.
